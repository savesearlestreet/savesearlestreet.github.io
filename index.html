In Brief: The Searle Street Story

1923: Faith & Home

Roman Catholic Church acquires the land for £1695 to house parishioners.

1974: Saving District 6

Cardinal McCann prevents expropriation, saving the cottages from Apartheid bulldozers.

1985: The Moral Trust

Land is transferred on the understanding it stay a trust for parishioners.

2014: Private Dispossession

The property is sold to a private developer, disregarding generations of care.

2026: The Final Order

A magistrate rules in favor of a hotel developer; families face removal.

Tenure Disparity

The Complete Chronology

135 Years: From Colonial Grants to Judicial Displacement.

1890 — 1923: Foundations

Colonial Origins

1890: Property granted to William Cairncross under Cape Quitrents.
1902: Transferred to William James Broadhurst.
1923: Father John Rooney purchases property for the Roman Catholic Church for £1695.

1928 — 1943: Stewardship

Arrival of the Founding Families

1928: The Savage grandparents move into No. 64.
1938: Mark Hutton’s Grandmother moves into No. 62.
1943: Bishop Francis Henneman transfers property to the Sisters of The Holy Cross for £2000.

1974 — 1978: Resistance

Defying the Group Areas Act

1974: Archbishop McCann appeals to the state; properties are exempted from District 6 replanning.
1978: Apartheid government attempts to rehouse tenants in Walmer Estate. Landlord asks tenants not to cooperate. Residents obey out of loyalty.

2011 — 2013: The Shift

Negotiations Collapse

March 2011: Landlord introduces written 3-month leases.
2012: Negotiator Moerdyk assures residents "all is well."
Aug 2013: Landlord suddenly pulls out of trust negotiations. Rawson Properties given mandate to sell.

2014 — 2026: The Displacement

The Era of the Private Owner

1 Sep 2014: Etienne du Toit purchases cottages for R2.395m.
2023: Rates default; residents pay Du Toit's debt themselves.
14 Jan 2026: Magistrate grants eviction. Vacate date: 28 February 2026.
